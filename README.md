# üîç Analysis & Key Findings
Looking at the dashboard you can see some clear differences in health outcomes around the world. Europe and Oceania generally have the highest life expectancy while Africa is still falling behind which highlights ongoing challenges with healthcare access and development. Countries like Japan, Switzerland and Australia show steady improvements over time which reflects strong healthcare systems and better living conditions.
Most countries have a fairly even gender balance although women tend to live longer. The dashboard also shows a link between BMI and life expectancy. In countries with higher BMI levels often wealthier nations generally live longer but only up to a certain point.
Overall these visuals make global health patterns easier to understand and highlight where progress has been made and where challenges remain.
